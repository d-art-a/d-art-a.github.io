Case Study

Engagement KPIs

Problem

Imagine you are a member based not-for-profit organisation, and one of your primary KPIs is how engaged your potential and paying members are with your online content. However, engagement is difficult to put numbers to, let alone track. In preparation for your monthly executive meeting, you check statistics on how members opened your newsletter, and how many likes and comments your social media posts received. Checking any more regularly than that is considered too much effort and doesnâ€™t seem to bring any benefit to the business.

Solution

daRta could build a solution to help your organisation track and report on your engagement KPIs. Imagine that, each night, your new business intelligence platform automatically connects to your membership database and all your outreach platforms to collect engagement data. This would include: Twitter, Instagram and Facebook for social media likes, shares, and comments; Google Analytics for website interactions; and Mailchimp for newsletter engagement. A sentiment analysis AI would categorise social media mentions, and the total reach of a post, including the networks of the people sharing it, is calculated. Now imagine that all this information is fed into an interactive report that is automatically sent to your inbox the day before your weekly meeting. Or, access the report on-demand for retrospective analysis of marketing and engagement strategies throughout the week.

Result

Your organisation now has visibility of your engagement KPIs throughout the month. With easier access to engagement data, discussions on the effectiveness of recent engagement and marketing strategies are now part of your weekly meetings. As a result, your organisation has since seen an increase in both the number of members and your engagement with members as your content becomes more refined and targeted.
